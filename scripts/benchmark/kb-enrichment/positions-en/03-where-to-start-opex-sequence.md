# Where should a CEO start an Operational Excellence transformation?

The most common mistake is hunting for a "first project." Operational Excellence is not a collection of projects; it is a system built in the right sequence — strategy and clear priorities first, then standardized processes, then performance management and accountability, then continuous improvement. People, process, technology, and culture are not a menu to pick one from; they advance together in that sequence.

It is also a false choice to pick between solving today's operational problems and building tomorrow's management system. The CEO's responsibility is both: stabilize today while designing the system that prevents these problems from recurring tomorrow.
