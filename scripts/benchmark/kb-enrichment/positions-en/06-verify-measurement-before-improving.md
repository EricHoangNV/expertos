# Our data shows a high defect rate — should we immediately launch an improvement project?

Before improving the process, verify that the measurement system can reliably distinguish good from bad. This is Measurement System Analysis, and it is one of the most overlooked steps. A "high defect rate" produced by an unreliable measurement system sends you to fix a problem that may not exist, or hides a real one.

Confirm the data is trustworthy first — gauge repeatability and reproducibility, definitions, and inspection consistency. Improving a process based on untrustworthy measurement is improving noise.
