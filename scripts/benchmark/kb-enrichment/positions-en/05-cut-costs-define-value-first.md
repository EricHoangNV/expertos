# Costs are too high — should we start a Lean waste-elimination program?

Not yet. Before asking where the waste is, ask what value the customer is actually willing to pay for. Waste is defined relative to customer value; without that definition you will "eliminate" the wrong things and cut into what actually matters to the customer.

Define value first, then remove what does not serve it. Cost reduction that is not anchored to customer value is just cutting, and it often destroys the very capability that earns revenue.
