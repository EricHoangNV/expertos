# People versus systems: how to think about it, and how to diagnose

Never choose between people and systems. Great organizations build systems that enable capable people to do their best work; systems and people are complements, not a trade-off.

To tell whether a problem is caused by people or by the management system, look at the pattern, not the person. One poor result may be an individual issue. The same failure repeated across different people, teams, or locations is a system problem — the system is producing the outcome regardless of who is in the seat. Start with the pattern, not the person: fix the system first, and only then address genuinely individual gaps.
