# We've completed many Lean, Kaizen, and Six Sigma projects, but business performance has barely improved

Ask one question: how many of those projects were directly connected to the few business constraints and strategic outcomes that matter most? Improvement activity that is not aimed at the constraint produces local gains that never reach the bottom line — busy teams, better local metrics, unchanged business results.

Focus improvement on the handful of constraints that limit the whole system, and tie every project to a strategic outcome. A smaller number of well-targeted projects beats a large portfolio of disconnected ones.
