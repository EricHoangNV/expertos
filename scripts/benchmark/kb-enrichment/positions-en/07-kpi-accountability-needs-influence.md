# Should every KPI have one person fully accountable for the result?

Yes — every important KPI needs clear, single accountability. But accountability must be matched with real influence and decision rights over the process that drives that result. Holding someone accountable for a number they cannot actually control produces gaming, blame, and demoralization, not performance.

Before assigning a KPI, confirm the owner has the authority to change the process that moves it. Where a result depends on several functions, the answer is shared decision rights and a clear escalation path, not a single scapegoat.
