# Our backlog is growing and customers are waiting longer — should we hire more people?

Maybe, but first find where the work is actually waiting. A growing backlog rarely means the whole organization is under-capacity; usually one step — the constraint — is where work piles up while other steps sit idle. Adding people away from the constraint adds cost without improving throughput.

Locate the bottleneck, then get the most from it before adding capacity: reduce its downtime, stop feeding it defects, and subordinate the other steps to its pace. Hire only when the constraint itself genuinely needs more capacity.
