# AI-OPEX: Automation Principles

AI and automation are amplifiers. They accelerate whatever process already exists — so applied to
a good system they multiply value, and applied to a broken one they multiply waste and errors. The
sequencing principles below keep AI adoption from making things worse.

## Simplify before automate
Do not automate a wasteful process. Automating waste just produces waste faster and locks the waste
into code. First remove non-value-adding steps and simplify the process; then automate what
remains. The cheapest, fastest automation is the work you eliminated.

## Standardize before automate
Automation requires a stable, standardized process to attach to. If the process varies from person
to person or day to day, automation either breaks or encodes one arbitrary variant. Standardize and
stabilize (bring into control) first, then automate the standard.

## AI adoption to transformation (maturity)
Value from AI grows through stages, and skipping stages fails:
1. **Adoption** — people use AI tools for individual tasks (drafting, summarizing).
2. **Integration** — AI is built into standardized processes and workflows.
3. **Transformation** — processes and business models are redesigned around AI capability.
Most organizations get stuck at adoption (scattered tool use) and see little enterprise value
because the underlying processes were never standardized or redesigned. Behavior change and process
redesign, not tool access, are what convert AI potential into realized value.

## Potential to realized value
The gap between AI's potential and realized value is closed by the operating system around it:
clean and available data, standardized processes, clear ownership, change management, and controls.
An AI pilot that works in a demo but is not embedded in a standardized, owned process delivers no
sustained value. Prioritize the AI portfolio by value and feasibility (data readiness, process
stability, risk), and scale the ones the system can actually absorb.

## Minimum viable data readiness
AI cannot outrun its data. Before scaling an AI use case, confirm a minimum viable data readiness:
the needed data exists, is accessible, is of adequate quality, and is governed. Chasing advanced AI
on unready data produces confident, wrong outputs.

## The rule
Fix and standardize the process, ready the data, then automate — and use AI to amplify a
well-designed system rather than to paper over a weak one.
