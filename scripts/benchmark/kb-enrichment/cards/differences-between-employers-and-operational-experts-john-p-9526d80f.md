# DIFFERENCES BETWEEN EMPLOYERS AND OPERATIONAL EXPERTS

- **Operational Mindset**: A key difference between regular operators and exceptional operational experts is the approach to problem-solving. Experts start by asking, "Hệ thống nào đang tạo ra vấn đề này?" (Which system is creating this problem?), shifting focus from symptoms to underlying structures.
  
- **System Design vs. Issue Resolution**: Exceptional experts prioritize designing systems over merely resolving issues, leading to sustainable solutions rather than temporary fixes.

- **Local vs. Global Optimization**: Experts transition from local improvements (each department optimizing its own performance) to global optimization, viewing the organization as an interconnected value chain. They assess whether decisions enhance overall operational efficiency.

- **Data-Driven Decisions**: Moving from personal experience to operational data is crucial. Experts question, "Chúng ta đang đo điều gì?" (What are we measuring?), ensuring that metrics reflect true operational effectiveness and support decision-making rather than just reporting.

- **Organizational Capability**: Experts focus on building organizational capabilities rather than relying on individual efforts. They design processes and standards that ensure stability and reduce operational risks as the organization scales.

- **CEC Certified Excellence Consultant Program**: This program emphasizes developing systemic thinking, operational thinking, and value creation thinking, enabling participants to view businesses from a deeper perspective and build sustainable operational capabilities.

- **Key Insight**: The mindset shift from problem-solving to understanding system dynamics is essential for operational excellence. This change can significantly reduce the number of issues an organization faces daily by improving the operational system itself.
