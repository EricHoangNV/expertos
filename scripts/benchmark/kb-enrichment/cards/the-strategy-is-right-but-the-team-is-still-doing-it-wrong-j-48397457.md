# THE STRATEGY IS RIGHT, BUT THE TEAM IS STILL DOING IT WRONG

- **Core Claim**: A correct strategy does not guarantee effective operational execution; the failure often lies in poor communication of the strategy.
- **Key Issue**: Leaders understand their goals, but teams often do not share the same understanding, leading to misalignment and inefficiency.
- **Communication Importance**: Effective communication is crucial; it must be clear, structured, and delivered with the right spirit to ensure everyone is aligned and motivated.
- **Consequences of Poor Communication**:
  - Misunderstanding of priorities.
  - Lengthy meetings without clear decisions.
  - Lack of consensus on leadership messages.
  - Resistance to necessary changes.
- **Effective Communication Outcomes**:
  - Teams understand what is important.
  - Smoother inter-departmental collaboration.
  - Shorter, more productive meetings.
  - Clear reception and implementation of messages.
- **Leadership Communication Skills**: Leaders must communicate in a structured manner, especially under pressure, to clarify, reassure, guide, and build trust.
- **Servic Tulit Program**: A training program designed for leaders and managers to enhance their communication skills, focusing on real business contexts such as conflict resolution, company-wide meetings, partner discussions, and public speaking.
